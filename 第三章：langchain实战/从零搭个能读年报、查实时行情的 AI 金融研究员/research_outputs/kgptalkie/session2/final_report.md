# Financial Comparison: Apple Inc. vs. Amazon.com, Inc. (2024)

## 1. Introduction
This report provides a detailed comparison of the financial performance of Apple Inc. and Amazon.com, Inc. for the year 2024. The analysis focuses on revenue, profitability, and key segment performance. Note that Apple’s data reflects its full fiscal year (ended September 28, 2024), while Amazon’s data covers the first three quarters of 2024 (January to September).

## 2. Apple Inc. Financial Overview (Fiscal Year 2024)
Apple's fiscal year 2024 demonstrated stable revenue growth and strong margin expansion, despite flat iPhone sales.

### Revenue Analysis
*   **Total Net Sales:** $391,035 million (a 2% increase from 2023) [1].
*   **Segment Breakdown:**
    *   **iPhone:** $201,183 million (approx. 51.4% of total sales).
    *   **Services:** $96,169 million (13% YoY growth), driven by the App Store, advertising, and cloud services [1].
    *   **Wearables, Home and Accessories:** $37,005 million (-7% YoY).
    *   **Mac:** $29,984 million (+2% YoY).
    *   **iPad:** $26,694 million (-6% YoY).

### Profitability Metrics
*   **Gross Margin:** $180,683 million (46.2% of net sales). This is an improvement from 44.1% in 2023, largely due to the high-margin Services segment which reported a 73.9% gross margin [1].
*   **Operating Income:** $123,216 million (Operating Margin of 31.5%) [1].
*   **Net Income:** $93,736 million. This was slightly lower than the previous year ($96,995 million) due to a significant increase in income tax provisions ($29.7 billion vs $16.7 billion) [1].
*   **Diluted EPS:** $6.08.

## 3. Amazon.com, Inc. Financial Overview (Q1–Q3 2024)
Amazon experienced robust growth in 2024, particularly in its high-margin AWS and Advertising segments, alongside a successful turnaround in international retail profitability.

### Revenue Analysis (9-Month Cumulative)
*   **Total Net Sales:** $450,167 million [2][3][4].
    *   Q1: $143,313 million
    *   Q2: $147,977 million
    *   Q3: $158,877 million
*   **Key Revenue Drivers:**
    *   **Online Stores:** $171,473 million.
    *   **AWS:** $78,770 million.
    *   **Advertising Services:** $38,926 million [2][3][4].

### Profitability Metrics (9-Month Cumulative)
*   **Operating Income:** $47,390 million (Operating Margin of approx. 10.5%) [2][3][4].
*   **Net Income:** $39,244 million.
*   **AWS Contribution:** AWS remains the primary profit engine, contributing $29,202 million (approx. 61.6%) of the total operating income for the first nine months [2][3][4].

## 4. Detailed Comparison

| Metric | Apple (Full Fiscal Year 2024) | Amazon (Q1-Q3 2024) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Total Revenue** | $391.0 Billion | $450.2 Billion |
| **Operating Income** | $123.2 Billion | $47.4 Billion |
| **Operating Margin** | 31.5% | 10.5% |
| **Net Income** | $93.7 Billion | $39.2 Billion |
| **Primary Profit Driver** | Services (73.9% Gross Margin) | AWS (35-38% Operating Margin) |

### Key Observations
*   **Revenue Scale:** Amazon's revenue scale is significantly larger than Apple's. In just nine months, Amazon generated $450.2 billion in sales, exceeding Apple’s full-year revenue of $391.0 billion.
*   **Profitability Efficiency:** Apple remains far more efficient at converting revenue into profit. Apple’s operating margin of 31.5% is triple that of Amazon’s (10.5%). Apple’s annual net income ($93.7B) is more than double Amazon’s 9-month total ($39.2B).
*   **Business Models:** Apple’s profitability is anchored by its Services ecosystem and high-end hardware margins. Amazon’s profitability is heavily dependent on AWS and its growing Advertising business, which offset the lower margins of its massive retail operations.
*   **Growth Trends:** Apple is seeing its strongest growth in Services (+13%), while Amazon is seeing consistent growth across AWS and Advertising, with a notable return to profitability in its International retail segment.

## 5. Conclusion
While Amazon dominates in terms of sheer revenue volume due to its retail scale, Apple remains the leader in profitability and margin efficiency. Apple's "Services-first" growth strategy is yielding record gross margins (46.2%), whereas Amazon's profitability is increasingly driven by the cloud (AWS) and high-margin advertising services, which successfully subsidize its logistics-heavy retail business.
